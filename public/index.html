Racism Against First Nations in Canada

First, we are starting off with what could be one of the most discriminatory pieces of legislation ever placed within the Canadian Government. This being The Indian Act, which was first introduced in 1867 (Reference 1).

This act has been pushed and pulled for change among the years due to the discrimination to the First Nations people within the piece of legislation itself. Although there are some parts of this act that give First Nations people certain rights and status. Just by the name it is overtly racist, claiming that the indigenous people of Canada are Indian, which is not true.

Throughout the years there has been many attempts to amend the act, one being in 1969, which called for the abolishment of the Indian Act via the White Paper (Reference 2). This was overwhelmingly rejected by the First Nations community as it stripped them of certain rights and freedoms. With all the negative backlash, this paper was withdrawn in 1971.

One disgusting law from the Indian act which stayed until 1951 was the following:

"Every Indian or other person who engages in or assists in celebrating the Indian festival known as the "Potlach" or in the Indian dance known as the "Tamanawas" is guilty of a misdemeanor, and shall be liable to imprisonment ... and any Indian or other person who encourages ... an Indian or Indians to get up such a festival or dance, or to celebrate the same, ... is guilty of a like offence ...(58) (reference 3)"

This is extremely discriminatory against First Nations people on the basis of one of their traditions. This type of oppression of culture by the Canadian government actually led to arrests across the West Coast of Canada. Even though this was in the past, from late 19th century into the mid 20th century.

But again, to reiterate the Indian Act still exists in the Canadian Government today. There have been many alterations and adaptions that have come along the times, but still regressive ideas remain. (Add more recent stuff here, must research about Bill S-3)

Racism Against Chinese Canadians in Canada

The Chinese Immigration Act of 1923 is another piece of Canadian legislation that shows the dark side of Canada past. On July 1st 1923 this act went into effect which banned Chinese immigrants from entering Canada unless they were a diplomat, student or under special circumstances.

Coincidentally, this was the same day as Dominion Day so the creation of this act became Humiliation Day for Chinese-Canadians and they refused to take part in any type of celebration at this time. Furthermore, this act did not only affect those from China, but any ethnic Chinese even those under British nationality as well. As taken from this it is strictly seen as an oppression of Chinese Canadians within Canada. This act was finally repealed in 1947 following World War 2 and the United Nations Charter of Human Rights, which Canada was a signatory.

Nonetheless, this act left a huge impact on Canada and its Chinese population as seen in the following image:

As you can see populations largely decreased while the act was in play, allowing no more Chinese immigrants to come to Canada. Not only did this affect those who wanted to immigrate to Canada, it largely affected those Chinese-Canadians who had family in China, who could not immigrate to Canada. Regardless of their citizenship, they were not allowed to return back to Canada which put a lot of emotional abuse on these families who were separated due to discrimination.
